# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People capturing fast thoughts — a phone note at 11pm, a pasted meeting summary at a bright desk — who then fire the result to a colleague as a link. No account, no setup. They want the page open and writable in under a second, and they want sharing to be one click.

## Product Purpose

ShareNotes is an accountless, instantly-shareable notes editor. Open a blank page, write (rich text, auto-saved), optionally lock it with a password, and share it as a link — editable or read-only. AI helpers (summary, tags, title, improve, continue, ask, translate, grammar) assist the writing. Success = the tool gets out of the way: zero-friction capture, trustworthy save, frictionless share.

## Brand Personality

Quick, trustworthy, unfussy. Warm but not cute; confident but not loud. The voice is plain and active ("New note", "Share link", "Saved"), never salesy. It should feel like a well-made everyday tool, not a marketing site.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS: Inter + cornflower-blue + white + gradient-blob hero + rounded-full pills. The default everyone ships.
- The 2026 AI cream/parchment look: warm-tinted near-white bg + serif display + terracotta. (The previous "Inkwell" pass leaned here — deliberately moving away.)
- Cold slate dashboards (slate-900/slate-50 everywhere).
- Decorative motion, rainbow-colored feature cards, emoji used as icons.

## Design Principles

- **The tool disappears.** Earned familiarity over novelty. Standard affordances; no invented controls for standard tasks.
- **Warmth in the accent, not the surface.** Pure-neutral surfaces; a single honey-amber carries identity and lives only on actions, selection, and state.
- **One material per job.** One type family for UI + prose; mono only for data (status, counts, dates, tags).
- **State is visible.** Every interactive element shows hover / focus / active / disabled. Auto-save status is always legible.
- **Restraint, with one moment of delight.** The home is the one brand beat (a highlighter-marker flourish); the editor stays calm.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large/UI glyphs ≥3:1, verified in both themes. Amber is a light hue — used as a fill with near-black text, or as a deeper "strong" variant for text/icons on light surfaces; never light-amber text on white. Visible keyboard focus rings. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored. Color never the only signal (icons + text accompany state).
